Introduction

Virtual reality (VR) is an environment of realistic-looking simulated objects or scenes.

The most common meaning refers to an environment generated by computer technology, which creates in the user the feeling of being immersed in it.

Said environment is viewed by the user through a device known as virtual reality glasses or headset.

This can be accompanied by other devices, such as gloves or special suits, which allow greater interaction with the environment as well as the perception of different stimuli that intensify the sensation of reality..

History Vr (*allways militar):(

The origin of virtual reality dates back to the Second World War. The United States Navy is in contact with MIT (Massachusetts Institute of Technology) for the possible creation of a flight simulator suitable for training bomber pilots. The project was called Whirlwind and its construction ended a few years later in 1951. It was not until 8 years later that the USAF (United States Air Force) took up the project under the name of “Claude Project” and a civil use of 3D technology appeared. .
